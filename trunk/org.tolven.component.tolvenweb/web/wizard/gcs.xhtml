The Glasgow Coma Scale comprises three tests: eye, verbal and motor responses. The three values separately as well as their sum are considered. The lowest possible GCS (the sum) is 3 (deep coma or death), while the highest is 15 (fully awake person).

Eye Response

Do not confuse "Eye opening to speech" with an awaking of a sleeping person which would score 4, not 3.

To induce pain, put pressure on the patient's fingernail bed; if no response then try supraorbital and sternal pressure/rub.
